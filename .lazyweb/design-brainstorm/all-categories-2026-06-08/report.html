Cross-pollinating creative UI concepts from leading photo editors and interactive gaming tools into GlamAI.

The Obvious Approach

Most AI styling apps (including the current version of GlamAI) offer a grid of static options. The user clicks a box (e.g. "Bob cut" or "Siren eyes") and runs the generation. There is no middle-ground customization or custom settings.

Cross-Pollination Ideas

Hairstyles Pre-Gen Length & Volume Sliders

Instead of selecting a static hairstyle template and getting a fixed output, users get slider controls to choose desired hair "Volume" and "Length" values (mapping to Prompt modifiers under the hood) before running the model.

FaceApp — Dynamic sliders and category filters adjusting localized features before generation. [Lazyweb]

The Pattern: Slider-driven parameter modulation mapped to generative prompt variables.

Why it's a Zag: Moves the playground experience from a basic "click-and-wait" template to an interactive virtual haircut studio, making it feel like a professional tool rather than a toy.

Makeup Discoverable Creator Preset Feed

Introduce a social discovery section inside the Makeup studio where users share custom-prompted makeup look presets (e.g., "Euphoria Sparkles with a Peach Lip"). Users can save, rate, and click "Try Preset" to apply the styling values instantly.

Facetune — Discover feed with custom look preset cards, before/after previews, and usage counters. [Lazyweb]

The Pattern: Community-driven preset gallery with one-tap configuration cloning.

Why it's a Zag: Solves prompt-block for users who want complex look outcomes but don't know what descriptors to type, while creating organic user engagement.

Face Scanner Live Biometric Landmark Finetuner

If the automatic facial mesh detection is slightly misaligned (due to lighting or angle), allow the user to drag the key vertices (eyes, lip boundaries, jaw outline) manually on their uploaded image to ensure 100% accurate structural compatibility calculations.

The Pattern: Manual adjustment overlays for algorithmic vector coordinates.

Why it's a Zag: Traditional scanners give a static report and fail if the photo is imperfect. Providing a manual vertex override builds user trust and makes the scanning technology feel incredibly advanced.

Retouch Spot-Retouch Canvas Brush ⚡ Wild Card

Instead of applying global filters, users brush over specific parts of their portrait (e.g., forehead wrinkles or under-eye circles) on the screen to paint localized retouch commands like "smooth" or "remove blemish."

The Pattern: Mask-painting editor integration with localized inpainting.

Why it's a Zag: Provides ultimate control over photo editing without washing out the entire image. However, it requires building localized canvas masking logic, making it a high-effort/high-reward Wild Card feature.
